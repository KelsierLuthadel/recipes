# Restaurant Style Ragu

![Restaurant Style Ragu](resources/restaurant_ragu.png)

*A slow cooked Italian meat sauce using restaurant techniques to develop deep flavour and a silky texture*

**Servings:** 4

**Ingredients**
- 2 to 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 500 grams minced beef or beef and pork mix
- 75 grams pancetta or bacon, finely chopped (optional)
- 1 onion, finely diced
- 1 carrot, finely diced
- 1 celery stalk, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 to 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 150 ml dry red or white wine
- 400 grams canned tomatoes or passata
- 300 ml stock or water
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- 1 bay leaf or small sprig thyme (optional)
- Pasta of choice
- 1 cup reserved pasta water
- 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil for finishing
- Parmesan cheese, grated

**Directions**
1. Heat a large heavy pan over medium high heat for 2 minutes. Add olive oil until shimmering.
1. Add half of the minced meat and cook for 6 to 7 minutes without stirring for the first 2 minutes, allowing deep browning to form. Stir occasionally once moisture evaporates. Remove and repeat with remaining meat. Set aside.
1. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion, carrot and celery and cook for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring every minute or so until softened and slightly sweet.
1. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
1. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 3 minutes until darkened in colour and slightly caramelised.
1. Pour in the wine and scrape the bottom of the pan to release browned bits. Simmer for 3 to 4 minutes until reduced by roughly half.
1. Return the browned meat to the pan. Add tomatoes, stock and optional herbs. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle simmer.
1. Reduce heat to low and cook at a bare simmer for 1 hour 45 minutes to 2 hours. Stir every 5 to 7 minutes during the first 30 minutes, then every 15 minutes afterwards. Add small splashes of water or stock if the sauce becomes too thick.
1. Taste and adjust seasoning after about 60 minutes of cooking.
1. Turn off heat and allow the sauce to rest for 10 minutes before finishing.
1. Cook pasta in well salted boiling water until 1 minute before al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
1. Add pasta directly into the ragu over medium heat. Add a splash of pasta water and butter or olive oil. Toss continuously for 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce emulsifies and coats the pasta.
1. Remove from heat, add grated parmesan and black pepper, rest briefly, then serve.

**Note**: Proper browning and slow simmering create most of the flavour. Finishing the pasta inside the sauce is essential for achieving a restaurant quality texture.
