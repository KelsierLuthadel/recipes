# Puris

**Makes:** 8 puris

**Prep time:** 30 mins + 15 mins resting

**Cooking time:** 5 mins (per batch)

## Overview
Celebratory deep-fried wholemeal flatbreads that puff up into soft, golden rounds. Puris are made with a tighter dough than rotis and are traditionally served at festivals, weddings, and special meals.

## Ingredients
### Dry
- 310 g (11 oz/generous 2 cups) wholemeal/whole-wheat flour, plus extra for dusting
- ½ tsp salt

### Liquid
- 100 ml (3½ fl oz/scant ½ cup) water, plus more as needed

### Frying
- Vegetable oil, for deep frying (about 2.5 cm/1 inch deep)

## Method

### Stage 1 – Make dough
1. In large bowl, combine flour and salt; make well in center.
1. Add a little water in well, mix and gradually add up to 100 ml water until dough forms.

### Stage 2 – Knead and rest
1. Turn dough onto floured surface; knead 5 mins until soft and smooth.
1. Cover with cling film/plastic wrap; rest in refrigerator 15 mins.

### Stage 3 – Shape puris
1. Divide dough into 8 pieces; roll each into ball.
1. Flatten, dust with flour, and roll into 2 mm (1/8 inch) discs.

### Stage 4 – Fry
1. Heat wok over medium; add 2.5 cm (1 inch) oil.
1. Test oil with breadcrumb; it should sizzle and brown.
1. Fry each puri one at a time, 40–50 seconds each side, until puffed and golden.
1. Drain on paper towels and serve immediately.

## Notes
- Tight dough and even thickness are key for puffing.
- Keep rolled puris covered while frying.
- Use ghee for richer flavor if desired.

## Serving
- Serve hot with chole (chickpea curry) or other festive dishes.
- Garnish with fresh coriander or a squeeze of lemon.

## Storage
- Best eaten fresh; may become chewy when cooled.
- Reheat briefly in hot oil or oven if necessary.
