# Rotis

**Makes:** 6–8 rotis

**Prep time:** 30 mins + 15 mins resting

**Cooking time:** 5 mins (per batch)

## Overview
Traditional unleavened wholemeal flatbreads eaten fresh across Indian homes. Properly rolled rotis puff up on a hot tawa and are finished briefly over an open flame for light, soft layers.

## Ingredients
### Dry
- 380 g (13½ oz/2½ cups) wholemeal/whole-wheat flour
- ½ tsp salt

### Fat
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil

### Liquid
- 240 ml (8½ fl oz/1 cup) water, or as needed

## Method

### Stage 1 – Make dough
1. In large bowl, combine flour, salt, and oil.
1. Add water gradually while mixing until a soft dough forms.
1. Knead 5 mins until smooth and elastic.
1. Cover with damp cloth; rest 15 mins.

### Stage 2 – Shape and roll
1. Divide dough into ping-pong-sized balls.
1. Flatten each ball; dust with flour.
1. Roll into even circles, about 1–2 mm thick.

### Stage 3 – Cook rotis
1. Heat tawa or nonstick pan over medium-high heat.
1. Cook roti 30–45 sec each side until light brown spots appear.
1. Transfer with tongs to open flame; puff each side briefly.
1. Avoid burning; remove once puffed and cooked through.

## Notes
- Even thickness is key for puffing.
- A heavy cast iron tawa works best.
- Rest dough for a more tender texture.

## Serving
- Serve immediately with curries, dals, or chutneys.
- Brush with ghee for extra richness.

## Storage
- Keep warm in a cloth-lined basket for up to 1 hour.
- Store cooled rotis in airtight container 1–2 days.
- Reheat on hot pan or wrap and microwave briefly.
